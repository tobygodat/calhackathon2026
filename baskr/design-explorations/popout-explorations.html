Pop-out — page centered, blurbs flanking

Same idea three ways: the rendered first page is the artifact in the middle; the classification (why) sits on one side, the paper itself (what) on the other.

A

Symmetric placard

— page framed in the centre of a quiet card; verdict left, bibliography right

Read as: a museum placard. The page is the object; the two columns hug it symmetrically. Calm, balanced, the card holds it together.

B

Annotated figure

— labels connect to the page with thin leader lines, like a figure callout

Read as: an annotated diagram. Each blurb points at the page. More "designed", a touch more chrome — the leader lines do the talking.

C

Lightbox captions

— no card; a large page floats with whisper-quiet captions and lots of air

Read as: a photo lightbox. Maximum restraint — the page is big, the text recedes, the abstract moves to a "Read paper" click. Most cinematic, least dense.
